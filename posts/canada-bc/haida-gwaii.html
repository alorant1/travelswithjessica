Haida Gwaii, British Columbia

July 2019

Home › British Columbia, Canada › Haida Gwaii, British Columbia

Skidegate

Taking advantage of the rain, we headed to Skidegate and the Haida Heritage Centre. This should be everyone’s first stop on a first visit to Haida Gwaii. After absorbing as much as we could about the culture here in Haida Gwaii, we popped into Ben Davidson’s gallery and got to chat with the artist himself. Nice that he sometimes collaborates with his dad, Robert Davidson. We are now tucked into the cozy Alaska View Lodge in Masset to reflect on the day.

Old Masset

From hippy culture and old boats to beautiful poles and phenomenal artists, tiny Old Masset has it all. Old Masset is one of two villages where the Haida regrouped after suffering severely declining numbers thanks to Smallpox. We were told that this little town raised an incredible number of world-class Haida artists, and we were fortunate enough to meet a few of them, all over the course of a few hours! We stumbled upon Jim Hart’s studio where he was working with his team on a a couple of remarkable and substantial commissions, we bumped into Corey Bulpitt, whose work already figures prominently in our home, and we met John Brent Bennett, an incredibly talented carver and print maker. Wow!

Tow Hill

Lots of outdoor time today with hiking, big trees and a beautiful sunset. Oh, and one more pole to share…

Tlell

Sitting at the beach in Tlell, an eagle perched on a spit, dusk settling in to the east, a picnic dinner of wine, cheese and hummus. A fire on the beach, gazing at a vast network of stars to cap it all off. Can you imagine a better late summer evening? Haida Gwaii is a quiet, magical place. Grid is patchy at best and that’s a good thing. The people here are so warm and welcoming. I’ll be back…
