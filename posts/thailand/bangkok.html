Bangkok, Thailand

December 2019

Home › Thailand › Bangkok, Thailand

The Grand Palace

The traffic in Bangkok! Seems to take the same amount of time to walk as to drive. Watching the Grab (Uber equivalent) driver’s approach on the app shows them painfully stuck. From the Grand Palace to a modern mall to street food, this is a city of contrasts. But if the traffic and humidity are enough to do you in, then the Thai people are the balance to bring you back. Happy, kind, warm and genuine.

Wat Pho

A precious metals day today. We visited Wat Pho, the so called Golden Temple and went to a silver-making workshop. During its construction in 1788, the king moved many Buddhas from abandoned temples and now Golden Buddhas abound here! It is also said to be the first university of Thailand and the birthplace of traditional Thai massage (yes, we did indulge!). While some of these Buddhas are world renowned, the 1,000 other Buddha images are certainly not to be overlooked!

Chatuchak Weekend Market

It’s Saturday in Bangkok. That means a trip to the Chatuchak Weekend Market, reputed to be the world’s largest weekend market. It was surprisingly quiet…until after lunch. As with most places, it’s best to go early. It’s quite a business with an average of 200,000 visitors per weekend poking around 15,000 stalls selling everything you can imagine including misspelled knock offs and, sadly, illegal wildlife. Capped the day off with a foot massage before dinner.

Wat Arun

On our last full day in Bangkok we visited Wat Arun, one of the iconic images of Bangkok. On the edge of the Chao Phraya River, this ceramic covered temple is also known as the Temple of Dawn, shining as the sun rises.
