Temples & Tuktuks

We are now in Northern Thailand and it feels a bit more like home…except for the heat, the temples, the tuktuks and more motorcycles and mopeds on the roads than cars. Ok. Let’s just say it’s a lot greener up here. Chiang Mai is 730 years old and the city boasts 300 temples (36 of which are in the very small old town). There are still remnants of the old city walls, aided in its day by a moat in keeping out the ancient intruders.

Doi Suthep

Today’s hike up Doi Suthep along the Monk’s Trail was through another tropical forest and carried us to another time. We came out of the forest into the most amazing temple grounds of Wat Pha Lat. It felt like a well-hidden sacred place that only a few have the luck of discovering. A much steeper trail then continued on to Wat Phra That. This temple at the top of the mountain is much more touristy.
