Sights, Sounds and Smells of Marrakech

After a 32 hour trip (including two 6 hour delays…don’t get me started on baggage transit efficiency), we made it to Marrakech! The sales tactics are different than the Souk in Jerusalem. It’s gone from “you will be my first sale of the day; you will bring me good luck” to “no Madame, it is closed that way, you must come this way”. One such diversion led us to a small, 15-family tannery cooperative. They kindly gave us a “Berber gas mask” which is a fistful of fresh mint… Fascinating process, all manual and all natural; who knew there was a market for pigeon poo! Missed opportunity at Granville Island. Oh and Adam is doing a great job making new friends!

The Medina

Old Town to New Town and the Perfect Blue

Today was spent exploring sharp contrasts in Marrakech. A leisurely walk took us from the Médina (Old Town) to the Guéliz (the New Town) and the Majorelle Gardens. A French painter, Jacques Majorelle, bought the huge palm grove in 1923 and designed the gardens around his studio which he painted in Majorelle Blue. After he died, it was abandoned and slated for demolition until Yves-Saint-Laurent saved the day by buying and renovating the gardens to what we see today. A quiet oasis in the hustle and bustle of the city with stunning colours and unbeatable style.

Ouzoud Falls

In light of the incident near Imlil in the Atlas Mountains, we abruptly changed our plans to visit that area and went to the Ouzoud Falls instead. 110m waterfalls (highest in North Africa, 2nd highest in all of Africa after Victoria Falls). And the cutest local monkeys. Abdelwahim was an amazing guide!
