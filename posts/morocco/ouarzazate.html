Ouarzazate, Morocco

December 2018

Home › Morocco › Ouarzazate, Morocco

Desert-bound

Today was mind-blowing! We began our desert adventure at 8am with the most wonderful guide, Mubarak. So far, we have passed through the high Atlas Mountains, reaching a peak in this area of 2260m (snow peaks in the distance. In January/February there will be enough snow to ski). We visited an argan oil women’s cooperative where we learned how argan oil is made. We stopped at a few Kasbahs (home/fort of the local leader, often built high on a hill for defense) on the way. One of the Kasbahs was owned by the “salt lord”, El Glaoui, and was situated on the trading route from the south. The traders passed through in search of salt in exchange for gold (equally valuable at the time). This guy was the main game en route and amassed a great fortune. It took 300 people three years to build his Kasbah (equivalent to $45 million labour cost in today’s dollars), which seriously rivals Vancouver’s real estate market! The Ksar at Ait-Ben-Haddou is a Berber castle located strategically along the main trade route between Sudan and the imperial cities of Morocco. Location, location, location! This ended up the site of many an epic movie location such as Laurence of Arabia, Gladiator and Cleopatra to name a few. We are now nestled in between the high and low Atlas Mountains at the most stunning Riad and will continue the journey to the Sahara tomorrow.
