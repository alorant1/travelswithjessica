Post 1: Big and Bustling Berlin

We certainly had a bit of size shock here. As the fifth largest city in Europe, Berlin felt enormous after nine weeks of mostly tiny to small towns. And after nine weeks, we were definitely in “chill” mode so we spent our time roaming around neighbourhoods and exploring the many memorials to the Holocaust and the Berlin wall. I appreciate the position Berlin (and Germany in general) has embraced as a reckoning with its sordid history. We can’t move forward if we don’t accept the past. The last time I was here was on MBA exchange in 1991, a scant two years after the wall came down. At the time, the psychological and cultural divide between the former east and west was palpable even to an outsider. There was resentment from both sides. I’m happy to see that the city now feels very much like one city with lots of neighbourhoods brimming with their own personalities. However, understanding the scary place this city had to navigate out of since the 1930s, I can’t help but be saddened by the many countries in the world heading down a similar, destructive path. How can the powers that be open this door again?
